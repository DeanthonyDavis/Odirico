A procurement-ready view of Odirico's consulting and software model.

Odirico is a Houston-based infrastructure brand built around two connected divisions: Odirico Consulting for field delivery and engineering support, and Odirico OS for software modules like PoleQA. 01

Company overview

This is the short-form narrative that can carry across outreach, proposals, capability statements, and early government conversations.

Odirico Consulting

Field engineering services, project services, construction QA/QC oversight, surveying support, and technical documentation for utility and infrastructure work.

Odirico OS

Operational software for inspection, reporting, and project coordination, starting with PoleQA as the first module.

Hybrid model

Consulting work sharpens the product roadmap, and the product side makes service delivery more structured and defensible.

Texas-first positioning

Built for utilities, cooperatives, contractors, municipalities, and infrastructure programs that need local context and field credibility.

Core competencies

Electrical utility infrastructure engineering

Competency 02

Project services and field coordination

Software-enabled inspection workflows

03

Differentiators

The strongest story in the document is not "we are a big SaaS company." It is that Odirico can combine field credibility with software built from the work.

Field expertise

Built around electrical utility infrastructure work rather than generic consulting language.

Proprietary software

Odirico OS and PoleQA give the firm a repeatable tool layer instead of relying only on third-party systems.

Texas-focused market fit

Houston-based positioning is useful for co-ops, MUDs, contractors, and public infrastructure buyers across Texas.

Services plus software

Most firms do one or the other. Odirico is stronger because each side improves the other.
